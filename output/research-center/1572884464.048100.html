# Steve Krouse 2019-11-04 08:21
I had a rambling train of thought based on Adriaan's pre recording idea: what is synchronicity good for? We can all pre record our talks and share them here for asynchronous feedback and questions, which can also be given and answered asynchronously. In fact the #share-your-work channel operates like this already!

So what are we hoping is different here? The main thing I see is that the social pressure of a due date will act as a forcing function, both to get us all to put our thoughts together on our projects, and to get more people bought in on giving feedback. Another benefit is seeing everyone's beautiful facial expressions and having some real human connection -- not to be underestimated for building community and collaboration.

Does this train of thinking resonate? Does anyone have suggestions to get the most out of synchronous and asynchronous methods?

One final comment: let's not let analysis paralysis derail this. If we can't come to agreement on a different plan in a couple days, let's go with Breck's plan as is

# Doug Moen 2019-11-04 08:55
"what are we hoping is different here?" The ideal for me is a symposium where everybody is physically present. There are live demos. There is a live Q&A after each demo. There is an opportunity to ask questions about the system being demoed, and have the presenter live-demo the answers to those questions. The proposed symposium format precludes all of these forms of interaction. And you can't have an interactive live-demo discussion afterwards on Slack. With no discussion, it isn't really a symposium. I think that the 2 minutes per presenter and no discussion format is too limiting. How about fewer presenters and more time per presenter, with discussion? Or maybe I should organize another symposium that follows these rules, because we can do multiple symposiums with different formats.
