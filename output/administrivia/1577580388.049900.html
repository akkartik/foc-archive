# Ivan Reese 2019-12-28 16:46
I (finally) tried Zulip for the first time today. Here are some initial impressions:

:small_orange_diamond: I too found the UI a bit clunky. Not nearly as rough as Spectrum, probably a bit better than Discourse, but not as good as Slack (or Discord). If we switched to Zulip, we'd be trading a bit of polish for a few more features. That's probably a good trade.

:white_check_mark: Linking directly to a topic, or to a message, seems to work just fine.

:small_orange_diamond: Topics being flattened into each stream seems like a mixed blessing, compared to the nested threads of Slack. If there are two intense conversations happening at the same time, the top-level stream view might become hard to navigate. There are some cases where two people spin a Slack thread up into the 100s of replies, which would also make the All Messages view hard to peruse unless you muted the noisy discussions. So it feels like you have more control, at the cost of needing to micromanage.

:white_check_mark: The performance seems good.

:white_check_mark: Searching the entire history of FoC is powerful!

:white_check_mark: Being able to import all existing history and users from our Slack is powerful!

I think I'd be willing to propose to the community at large that we make the switch. If anyone watching here feels slightly or strongly that we shouldn't, now would be a great time to express those feelings.
