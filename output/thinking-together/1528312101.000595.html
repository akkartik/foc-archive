Text (CLI) is human and machine-readable but I would say it's human-first data. You can write your stuff and most people can understand that but for computer it's just list (or grid) of meaningless numbers.

# William Taysom 2018-06-08 21:25
@Niko Autio Hmm. Conventional shell CLIs combine several features that can be tweaked independently. Part of it is that you have a dialog, a transcript of the dialog, the size of the exchanges in the dialog (commands and outputs). Another part is representation (concrete and abstract). Concretely, CLIs are text heavy if not text exclusive. (Of course you have have a text-based UI that isn't a CLI https://appliedgo.net/tui/.) And there is a question about how readily to can get at the text that you see on the screen.
# William Taysom 2018-06-08 21:30
Abstractly, you can have all kinds of things going on. Yes, any command will have more or less syntax and the output will appear as more or less as structured text, but that doesn't mean that the output is text. For example, just behind this Slack window, I have Pry running https://github.com/pry/pry. The command language is Ruby (text), but the output made of Ruby objects. Yes, they get printed, but I can do more stuff with them than the output of a UNIX shell command.
