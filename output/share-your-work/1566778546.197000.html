# Tim Babb 2019-09-04 12:41
My preferred representation for a rectangle is none of thoseâ€” I find it cleaner to work like: struct Rect { vec lo, vec hi }, where lo and hi respectively hold the smallest and largest coordinates along each axis. Then the logic for a rect collapses to range arithmetic (i.e. unions, intersections, translations, etc. are natural using max/min, including with empty rectangles).

This also admits "signed" rectangles which may contain their interior or exterior, if that makes sense for your application (otherwise, lo > hi can be considered "empty").

I have also found it advisable that the range be inclusive (i.e. both endpoints are considered part of the rectangle) to avoid special cases and "preferred coordinate frames" where some edges of the rectangle are "special". This is somewhat contrary to typical range conventions in programming, but I've found from experience that it saves headache.

That presentation also generalizes to higher dimensions nicely.
