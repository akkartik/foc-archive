# Jarrett Atkinson 2020-11-13 10:59
It's rather long, but the high points are
• Online textbooks are bad paper on screens, why not take advantage of the new hardware underpinnings
• Roam is fantastic, and everyone should use it. Why are textbooks linear anyway?
• Examples are the way to go, and parameterized examples are better. Live examples are best.
• (e.g. physics book where every single concept was encoded in a math language in the book, and the examples had clear dependencies to the formulas defined in the book)
• What happens when each function in the book has its own page with examples, definitions, code, worked math problems, etc...
• Allow users to put additional material in the book to expound on their thoughts
• Crowd source better material over time - books don't need to be static. Look at wikipedia.
• Everyone learns different, so why force all to use the same book? Why not have multiple versions of each page tailored to different learning types?
