Mathematica has long been a pretty unique achievement. The notebook interface, the symbolic power, and the polished consistency of it all. Due to being closed-source, I've somewhat ignored their evolution, and you need some tolerance to S.W.'s self-aggrandizing tone :wink:. > So for example, a computational language can intrinsically talk about things in the real world—like the planet Mars or New York City or a chocolate chip cookie. A programming language can intrinsically talk only about abstract data structures in a computer.
