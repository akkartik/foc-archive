# Andrew Noble 2025-11-30 14:27
Hello!

I'm Andrew. I grew up in Alaska and have found my way to computing via mechanical engineering in undergrad, where the few programming classes I took fascinated me. I previously worked on strawberry harvesting robots and hiked across the U.S. on the Pacific Crest Trail, among many other cherished outdoor adventures.

These days I am in a masters program for robotics at Carnegie Mellon, and enjoy tinkering on random creative projects (including woodworking and sewing in addition to programming) and going down internet rabbit-holes, much like the one that led me to this slack group today!

Most of my programming projects thus far have been centered around web technologies, though I yearn to get back into some low level stuff like the Assembly I so enjoyed in college. Some of my projects can be found on my website. Recently, I have been experimenting with giving LLM's a "whiteboard" in the form of SVG diagrams, with which they can better convey spatio-visual STEM topics like fluid mechanics, physics, or electronics.

As someone from the first smartphone-addled generation (gen z), I resonate a lot with the thinking of Brett Victor and others, like Jaron Lanier, about the gulf between what technology is vs how it could be.

I am looking forward to engaging with this community and meeting thoughtful technologists from around the world.

All the best.
