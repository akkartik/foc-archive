# Mike Austin 2023-10-17 14:52
I see there is a prominent "amiga-tick" reaction available for messages. I loved my Amiga 2000 and the Amiga community. What does it mean when you use the amiga-tick reaction?
# Ivan Reese 2023-10-17 16:23
To me, :amiga-tick: means exactly the same as :cake: or :+1: or :heavy_plus_sign: or :100: or :heart: or :white_check_mark: or :heavy_check_mark:. It's just a way to signal a positive reaction. Choosing to use one or another in any given situation is a matter of personal taste and style.
# Kartik Agaram 2023-10-17 16:48
Kartik's glossary:
• :heart:: I love this, nice work. (Usually for demos.)
• :100:: Absolutely agreed. (Usually in debates.)
• :heavy_check_mark:: Done. (Usually in my work Slack.)
• :+1::skin-tone-3:: Acknowledgment. I like this, but not enough to throw you a :heart:
• :cake:: Ivan got here before me, and I couldn't work up the energy to find the right emoji.
# Ivan Reese 2023-10-17 17:12
Additionally:
• :eyes: : I will take a look (or already did), and don't have anything more to say yet
• :clap: or :tada:: I celebrate this, high-five, nicely done.
• :joy: or :sob: or :hatching_chick:: Damn, that sucks, sorry. (I use hatching chick for this whenever there's an element of naivety, a la f-around find out.)
• :beers:: Thank you for doing that thing that I probably should have done.
• :expressionless:: I despise this but can't be arsed to dignify it with a powerful reaction.
• :thread-please:: Hey, this is a cool thought, you should start a whole new thread about it. (Kudos to @Kartik Agaram for setting up this particular emoji+meaning. Also note that the :thread-please: emoji used to have a different meaning — hey, this top-level post is a reply to the top-level post above it, and you should use the threaded reply feature — but I've personally decided that a quick DM is a better way to handle that situation)
