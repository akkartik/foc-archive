# Pete Millspaugh 2024-03-05 06:46
It's pretty neat how current this book reads being published in 1993. I like Nardi's counter examples of driving cars and playing music as alternatives to natural language ("mundane conversation") as the default medium of human machine communication.

Reminds me of something I read recently in Make It Stick—that there is no empirical support for the claim that instruction in one’s preferred learning style (e.g. "I'm a visual learner") improves learning outcomes. It's the subject matter that dictates the best medium, e.g. diagrams for geometry, words for poetry

# Don Abrams 2024-03-12 06:54
Make it stick was pretty focused on learning/retention rather than communicating/understanding.
In teaching/communicating, understanding the shared context is needed to maximize efficiency (which is why tutoring/sales generally has better outcomes than class/marketing)

Related, I think we can talk about how code works with lower precision because we have a lot of shared context, which she stressed in other parts of that chapter. We'd have very different conversations with someone non technical (different shared context and probably a different model).
