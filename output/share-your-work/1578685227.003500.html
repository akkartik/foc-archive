# yoshiki 2020-01-11 17:03
Personally I like this more than the previous one with the illustrated character. But I think there are still some weaknesses in the delivery compared to the product.

What interests me most about Storybook is that you can create code that uses many different services without having to write a custom integration or find a suitable API client library(which may or may not be an official one). I feel when writing such code, the integration-specific parts tend to dominate the complexity and a lot of effort is duplicated across projects. Being able to outsource all that to a provider dedicated to keeping the integrations working and high-quality seems like a great convenience that people would pay money for.

I feel like this aspect isn't really conveyed well to someone unfamiliar with the product right now.

Also compare to the landing pages of Zapier or IFTT, which much more concisely(and visually) explain the value that the product provides, rather than mockups or screenshots of what the product looks like(which I don't think are very helpful for a landing page, though in storybook's case having some code snippets might be nice):

https://ifttt.com/
https://zapier.com/

To me Zapier's:
> Easy automation for busy people. Zapier moves info between your web apps automatically, so you can focus on your most important work.
feels more evocative and concrete than:
> Storyscript is a language, editor, database, infra and community all-in-one. Create holistic apps, tools and workflows blazing fast that power your team
which while more descriptive is also more plain and only highlights one rather vague benefit("blazing fast". It also isn't clear here to me what is blazing fast). Compare to Zapier highlighting two concrete ones("easy", "focus on your most important work").

In any case I think the product is exciting and I believe it has a lot of potential, so good luck!
