# Ivan Reese 2019-08-17 11:33
core.async is a bit of an oddity. It was (IIRC) mainly the project of Tim Baldridge, who is no longer at Cognitect and no longer working on it. It was never properly finished, and has a bunch of bugs and shortcomings. I think there have been a handful of community projects that have done a better job at cracking the same nut.

I'm not sure that I'd describe it as message passing. It's CSP, which is a more specific thing. If you say that CSP is a form of message passing, then you might as well lump in the actor model and Pi-Calculus and a few other things, and at that point a lot of meaning has been lost. After all, the interesting thing in core.async isn't the messages & inboxes, it's the use of queues and the coordination mechanisms.
