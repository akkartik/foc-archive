# David Piepgrass 2019-04-15 07:37
"the Commit" - what about CVS? SVN? Even SourceSafe? They conceptualized version control differently but they did most of what people needed. Versioning was the really important thing, not git-style commits. Git was invented and designed for a huge software project (Linux) and it was only created because the BitKeeper source control system that Linux previously relied on was no longer free to access. Many people ignored Git until the rise of GitHub, and I ignored Git until a good GUI was available for it (Git Extensions).
