# Ivan Reese 2021-03-17 09:47
#present-company was chosen to fill 2 needs, both relatively low-importance but still valuable enough to justify existence.

1. This is very much a community of programmers (by accident of history if not by essence), so we're going to want to talk about programming stuff beyond just speculative futurism. We need a channel to talk about programming of the present.
2. This is very much a community of people (see earlier parenthetical :), so we're going to want to talk about stuff beyond just programming. We need a channel to share and discuss our other interests. (It could easily be argued that this is a superset of need #1.)
Thus, #present-company is a pun, so that it can (awkwardly, yes) describe both needs.

• #the-present has the same problem as #present-company — people who aren't already fully familiar with the purpose of the channel won't be able to intuit it just from the name. So it's a lateral change, not an improvement.
• #present-of-coding covers need #1 very well, but doesn't do anything to help intuit need #2. Again, lateral.
• #random, #general, and #off-topic were other popular suggestions. They only address need #2. #general creates confusion with #thinking-together. #random and #off-topic imply that the channel is for low-value posts, and I don't like that. Any of these would be worse than #present-company
Stefan's nested boolean logic is the best expression of my personal intent for / understanding of these channels that I've seen so far. I'd previously been thinking of the channels as a triangle, but it was hard to establish orthogonal axes. I might want to steal that and put it in the handbook, though with one change: isAboutOneMainLink (or something clearer) rather than hasLink, since links are sometimes useful as reference material when writing a #thinking-together post.

One other thing to note here is that the logic for choosing a channel is meant to be quite fuzzy. If something could go in two or three of these channels, it can go in any of them. The only relevant failure mode is when many posts are going in channels where they clearly don't belong. We don't need optimality, we just need sufficiency.
