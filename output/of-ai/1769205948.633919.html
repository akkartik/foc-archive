# William Taysom 2026-01-23 14:06
❯ Tell me please what did you learn from the exercise?

:black_circle_for_record: What I Learned

1. Reward function design > algorithm sophistication
We tried random evolution, CMA-ES adaptive search, various population sizes. None of that mattered as much as getting the reward function
right. Survival=500, win=300 produced passive bots. Win=1000, damage×3 produced killers.

2. Gradient matters for learning
When bots couldn't win against the gauntlet, scores were 0 - no gradient to climb. Adding survival points and damage rewards gave the
optimizer something to improve even when not winning. But the balance had to favor winning over merely surviving.

3. Parametric vs structural search
Evolution found good parameter values within our bot template. It couldn't discover entirely new strategies like corner camping - we had to
add corner_affinity to the genome manually. Structure came from us; tuning came from evolution.

4. Training environment stability
Random subsets of opponents = noisy signal = stalled learning. Consistent gauntlet = stable signal = progress.

5. The one-chronon delay is everything
Rubowar's core mechanic (sensing happens one tick before you can act on it) makes prediction and evasion crucial. The evolved bots learned to
stay at range and juke - playing the delay.
