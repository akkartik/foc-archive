# Kartik Agaram 2019-03-22 11:37
There's already lots that people aren't used to. When they're in a strange codebase, that's going to be the norm. Some number of people will poke at something and withdraw after realizing it requires a larger investment than they expected. Nothing one can do about that. (And indeed those two categories are often the same people, just shifted in time. I have to recoil a few times from a codebase before I internalize what I need to do and why and make a larger commitment.)

The metric for learning new things is often "how familiar is it?" when I think it should be "how parsimonious is it?" I think a codebase that relies consistently on 5 unfamiliar mechanisms/concepts/names/whatever is superior to one that relies on 100 familiar whatevers.
