# Ray Imber 2020-07-17 15:11
This may be a weird comparison, but I'm reminded of things like prison art, trash art, electronic music, and particularly chip tunes (a sub-genre a I particularly love.... much :heart: for Koji Kondo and all the early Nintendo music designers.) Much of the beauty and history of electronic music is tied to people trying to imitate "real" music using limited technologies.

Society constantly underestimates how creative humans can be given even what is considered "bad" tools. "Proper education" and "Proper tools" are often overrated. Really those things are more about virtue signaling and societal gate keeping, not creativity.

People are often at their most brilliant when they have limitations. I think this applies to children even more so. “The enemy of art is the absence of limitations.” - Orsen Welles.

lol, It's true, but I don't think it's necessarily a bad thing. I think about it this way. A great musician usually doesn't start by making original music immediately. They learn by imitating existing masters and playing pre-written music first. In painting they do similar things with "Masters Studies". They literally try to break down and reverse engineer masters paintings. "New original ideas" aren't created in a vacuum. Surrealism happened as a response to realism. Cubism was a response to surrealism... (Sorry, my inner art history nerd is showing... :stuck_out_tongue: ).

TLDR; I think nostalgia is totally cool and not a bad thing, and I'm also excited to see how games evolve with the next generations.
