# Andrew F 2026-05-05 14:53
I find getting/keeping on top of all discussions is more a problem of raw volume than the software. Small discord servers are easy to keep up on. With large ones, like most popular community servers, I don't bother trying, and instead pick a few channels to focus on. But that was true old-school forums, too. I recommend disabling most notifications.

For a new community, I don't know, maybe one of the nice XMPP servers. That's more viable than average for a tech community in particular. They're trying to make it happen again. https://xmpp.org/software/?category=servers&cscore=advan… I remember hearing good things about Prosody as a relatively turn-key setup, but ejabberd is obviously one of the classics. # dman-os 2026-05-06 00:42
Mute every server by default and enable notifications selectively on certain channels. Most servers have dedicated announcement roles that go through the "Mute" so you won't miss important bits.
# dman-os 2026-05-06 00:43
One issue with this though is that threads you started or participated in are muted by default. You'll need to remember to enable notifications for a thread at the top right.
# dman-os 2026-05-06 00:45
Additionally, there's an inbox panel at the top right in the title bar.
# dman-os 2026-05-06 00:50
Another problem is that if you're talking in a channel that's muted, you might miss a reply if some neglects to to mention you by replying to your message. So if not using threads (sometimes they're too ceremonious), just ensure to unmute a channel before ever writing in one and mute it back when done.
