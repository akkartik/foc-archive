# Tom Larkworthy 2026-02-02 10:59
yeah I am hoping the reactive runtime gives it an uber-tight feedback loop. The feedback is the environment, not the training data. I often see the agent initially cannot call its own tools properly at first, but after round 10 it is warmed up and very productive. The early failures are because I have not optimized the prompt yet, its often completely out-of-whack with what I have actually implemented, but also, I don't really need to, coz the environment is the strong feedback signal.

Yes its also pretty bad at overall architecture, but it is great at copying established patterns. Robocoop3 itself is so much better than my previous LLM project, written in a tenth of the time coz I figured out how to get it it to port the agent loop from OpenCode.

Most of my prompts are: Look at X, Y Z, now do it in C. Usually X, Y and Z is something I wrote a few years ago. OpenCode port was the first time I ported 3rd party code, its MIT licensed and I attribute it. I think that unblocks a ton of options of remixing. So unless every aspect of a project is totally rethought from the ground up, I think you can actually very easily make a game if you have a game that has somewhat the right architecture in place.
