# Konrad Hinsen 2023-04-03 02:29
There are two technologies that I'd expect to be useful in the context of LLMs:
1. Controlled natural langues (see https://sites.google.com/site/controllednaturallanguage/ for a list), which are subsets of natural languages that can be assigned formal semantics.
2. Concatenative programming languages (https://concatenative.org/wiki/view/Concatenative%20language), which are formal language whose syntax shares the linear structure of natural languages that is the basis of LLMs.
Has anyone seen such connections pointed out or explored yet?
