# Kartik Agaram 2018-10-06 09:01
One conclusion I reluctantly arrived at after taking to lots of people: sometimes a vision being too broad can make it unpersuasive. Most laypeople are just not used to thinking so big. And the farther a programmer is from a sub-field, the harder it is to care about huge upheavals in it.

If you're working on a piece of a piece, maybe focus on it and explain how it may be useful without/before the rest.

(Because if it isn't useful by itself, maybe you aren't working on the right sub-problem.)
