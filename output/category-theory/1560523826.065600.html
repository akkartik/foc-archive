# Stefan Lesser 2019-06-14 07:50
Reading the philosophical discussion it occurred to me that thanks to the abstract / general nature of category theory, there are as many different reasons why people are interested in it. I'm interested in it for actually quite practical reasons. Thought I'll list a few here, to provide a counter-balance to the more philosophically themed discussion (which is also valid, just not my cup of tea):

• There's the connection between type systems and categories. As I mentioned in the call, category theory felt like finding the missing pages of the Haskell manual to me. Suddenly a lot more of the type stuff in Haskell made so much more sense to me, e.g. types as set of (possible) values, abstract data types and their composition, higher-kinded types, etc. For me a lot of things I thought I understand suddenly "clicked"; I had a lot of valuable "Aha!" moments that I'm sure make me a better functional programmer.
• As @William Taysom pointed out during the call, there's a super-tight connection between (relational) databases and categories. The Seven Sketches book introduces categories using this connection in chapter 3. They allude to (and there is further research from one of the authors) that category theory provides a formal way of transforming one relational database schema into another, which I find very useful for building more flexible universal data models, which is one of the things I'm exploring currently.
• Graphs can also trivially be converted into (free) categories, which means there is a visual representation that comes with denotational semantics out-of-the-box. Which somehow seems interesting to me, if you're also into visual programming languages… that's also what the Graphical Linear Algebra blog is rooted in.

Overall there appears to be a lot of power in Functors — the "magic" behind your friendly map() function in your favorite programming language, except that that's just one incarnation of the concept, and it seems to work in lots of other contexts too. I want to understand this well enough to be able to apply this to other domains.

Take all this with a grain of salt, as I'm still learning and might not really understand what I'm talking about…
