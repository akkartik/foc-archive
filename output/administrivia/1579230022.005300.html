# Ivan Reese 2020-01-17 08:35
@Scott Werner @Mariano Guerra A handful of people have asked for us to move to both a forum and a chat (whether Slack or Zulip or otherwise). They often couple that with the suggestion of finding some way to synchronize activity between them, some sort of bot or something. Nobody has actually gone past the ideating stage with that proposal, though — no proof of concept for what that synchronization would look/feel like. My concern with centering the community on two different platforms is that... we're already such a small community. This Slack can sometimes go for a few days without any activity. If we split the community across two platforms, I feel like one of them would quickly fizzle out and the people who preferred that one would not come back to rejoin the rest of the group.

There is already a Reddit group that is very, very closely aligned with this FoC slack — r/nosyntax. Rather than creating a FoC subreddit in addition to whatever chat or forum we settle on, perhaps we could just strike up some sort of official partnership with the mods of nosyntax, and cross promote each other. Then, rather than running a sync bot (which would surely be very noisy and people would just ignore it), folks who participate in both communities would cross-post any particularly interesting conversations.
