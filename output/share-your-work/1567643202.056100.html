# Kartik Agaram 2019-09-09 16:39
I've been thinking about this comment for a while.

Definitely agree with the second half. I too push back on Edward here.

But I think you missed my point in the first half. The fact that engineering resources are rarely available should cause us to question if the academic model is sufficient for software research. The constraints are different:

• We don't need much up-front infrastructure. A single person can make progress on projects in off-hours.
• Longitudinal studies are exponentially more valuable than anything else.
• We can't just observe the world. We have to introduce new artifacts into the world and observe how the world reacts and adapts to them over a long period.

So I don't think your statement is a defense of academia at all. I think it's an indictment, narrowly when it comes to computer science academia.
