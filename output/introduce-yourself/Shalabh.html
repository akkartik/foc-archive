# Shalabh 2019-03-24 23:29
Hi Everyone,

It was great to meet some of you today at the SF/Dynamicland meetup. While I've
been on Slack for a bit, I never posted to introductions so here it is.

I'm a software engineer professionally and over time I've grown increasingly
dissatisfied with the general state of affairs. It was all fun and games for a
while, but then I started watching Alan Kay videos :smile:. While digging into
historical ideas and banging my head against what seem to be a hard ceiling in
many of the tools and systems we use, I've arrived at the position that there is
a large space of possibilities that has been left unexplored while we continue
to incrementally hammer away at the entrenched systems. I believe big leaps can
only happen if many established fundamental ideas are changed or replaced. For
instance, we store and share information by encoding it in a
non-introspectable, very low level medium (aka files) that also loses its
connections to related information, history and identity. We then redesign these
features over and over in various ways in different contexts. Programs as text
files is another poor representation. We re-implement the same functionality and
schemas in different siloed processes and services. It seems our language for
interconnection and scaling up is really poor. Much of programming deals with
stuff that isn't really computing anything but just dealing with represenation
changes (e.g. parsing - doesn't change the information content). Worse, this
type of represenation shuffling code is tightly intermingled with the computing
code. We sit in front of powerful computers, and simulate them in our heads -
humans are simulating the general purpose simulator.

So what's an alternative? I don't know but perhaps creating a new
PL/DB/framework to fit in a PL/DB/framework shaped hole in the current mess may not
help. We need to explore ideas that are holisitic, ground up rethinking of what
computing is, how the space is partitioned into concepts and artifacts (note
files/filesystems, OS processes, databases, 'programs', compilers etc. are all
means to an end, serving the users achieve some of their goals - what are the
goals and what other ways can they be expressed?). How does something like this
'alternative substrate' or 'new material for computing' fit onto the existing
world? It doesn't, but my interest is more of a research exercise - how can it
be done, what would make it complete and self sustaining? If we have some good
ideas, we can prototype. Since this is broad and can go in all directions, I've
been writing to try and clear my thoughts and keep moving forward in some way.
