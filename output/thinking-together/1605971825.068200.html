# Jack Rusher 2020-11-22 02:18
The IETF way was predicated on (1) the existence of a working reference implementation (no arguments about possible futures without working code) and (2) our ability to disagree productively until we reach some kind of general consensus, meaning we didn't need to all agree, but that the community more or less settled on a way forward. None of this made flame wars completely non-existent, but it was an extremely useful approach when dealing with a wide variety of engineering problems back in the days.

(This isn't important for "let's dream the future" discussions, but is very important when it comes time to herd the cats into shipping software.)
