Humane Tech Community:

https://community.humanetech.com

What I like about this is that it's mostly driven by stakeholders OTHER than programmers, who have recognize the need to improve the state of software / programming. That's invaluable perspective, since us tech guys are sometimes biased by our own needs vs how software can be changed to better serve humanity (business, users). I'm very hopeful that bringing more of "us" into their org may have great potential to bridge that gap and do the most good

# Ivan Reese 2019-02-12 08:45
Do we really want a hub-and-spokes model? That sort of centralization is very "cathedral". Me, I prefer to embrace the distributed essence of the web, and say that a webring/graph of interlinking sites is fine. Sure, you won't have all of these projects under one beautifully painted dome roof, but you also won't run into the problem of needing to decide what belongs and what doesn't (eg: I like to link to video games). And besides — nobody has time to actually read all this stuff and make programming tools of the future, right? (Not just me?)
