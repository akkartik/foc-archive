# Stefan Lesser 2019-05-25 04:21
Apart from maybe eventually moving somewhere else, I think we could improve the situation here in this Slack today if we agree on one small change: even if it's crappy, let's use Slack's threading more! If you are responding to something someone posted directly into a channel, please create a thread.

Think of a post directly into a channel as somebody creating a new discussion thread in a proper forum.
Consider posting to the channel as something that's “higher cost” — it should open a new discussion and needs to be potentially interesting to everyone in the channel. Everything that's related to an existing discussion might not be interesting to those who are not interested in that particular topic, so let's not pollute the channel with those responses and put them in a thread.
And for those situations where a new discussion emerges out of a thread, there's the “also post to channel” feature.

I don’t mean this in the sense of strict rules. This stuff can only work if most of us can see the value of this and are willing to help encouraging others to do it. Maybe I’m wrong and it's a stupid idea — let me know, that's okay. I think it minimizes the problem of interesting stuff scrolling past too quickly, makes channels easier to browse, and it fixes notifications for me, because I subscribe to all threads I find interesting.

Ah, I’m talking to mostly programmers, so let me put it like this: take advantage of scope, more local variables, less global state!
