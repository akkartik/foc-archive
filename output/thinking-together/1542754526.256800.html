# Duncan Cragg 2018-11-21 01:08
Well there's pros and cons. In the world of possible things you can write to represent values, bools and numbers are only two value types in a much wider range of values, all of which we probably fall back onto strings to represent. By starting with strings you generalise to "it is what it looks like" - if it parses as a number, it's a number, and you can now define a universe of possible regexps to declare in fine grain any other type of value. By reducing to the simple "type = syntax", it could make the logic you're looking for easier in some way. Or maybe not. Dunno what you're looking for really!! :smile:
