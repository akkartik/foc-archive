# Yoz Grahame 2019-03-24 14:19
(frantically throws together some kind of intro before heading to meet everyone at Dynamicland)

Hello there! I’m Yoz, a short bald Brit who lives in Oakland and works in SF. I’ve been lurking on this Slack for a while after enjoying a few episodes of Steve’s podcast. Some relevant bits of history:
1. Ever since I first logged onto LambdaMOO in 1993, I’ve been obsessed with the potential for shared programmable worlds. This led me to 5 years working at Linden Lab, the creators/maintainers of Second Life, Blocksworld and Sansar, all of which are shared programmable worlds like MOO. It taught me a huge amount about the underestimated benefits and problems of these systems.
2. I spent 4 years at 18F, a consultancy within the US Government. It teaches departments about agile development & procurement, user-centered design and open source, all with the aim of serving citizens better. Most of my time was spent both programming new systems and learning about legacy ones. It convinced me that most of our software development was inherently a bad idea, and that it would be better to build on no-code/low-code systems, and to focus on empowering staffers rather than automating their jobs away.
3. I’m fascinated by debugging, with existing and new tools. I’ve given talks on debugging (see thread) and played around with creating new debugging tools.
4. These days I’m something of a software pessimist, to the point where I’m not sure that we should be teaching kids to code at all - at least, not until we are able to sufficiently manage the complexity we create.
5. I love amazing bug stories - see the thread for a couple, and also for my favourite computing paper.

Looking forward to meeting and chatting with folks!
