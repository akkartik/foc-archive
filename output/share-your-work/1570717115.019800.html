# Ivan Reese 2019-10-10 18:23
I'm building Hest as a simulation / modelling tool for my company, which makes hydraulic and electrical learning media here: https://www.lunchboxsessions.com — but our simulations aren't meant to be true to reality; we vary the degree of strict correctness as needed to illustrate a given point. Very different from something like Automation Studio or the stuff coming out of Festo Didactic.
# Ivan Reese 2019-10-10 18:24
The programming model of Hest is designed to be a bit of a generalization of electrical and hydraulic schematics.
# Ivan Reese 2019-10-10 18:43
The insight that led to this design is that these schematic representations are very visually and conceptually similar to a node-and-wire visual programming language. So with a bit of effort you could make something that worked like a general purpose programming language, but also felt immediately familiar to people who work with schematics.
# Ivan Reese 2019-10-10 18:44
Like Will said — this is at a different level of abstraction from your original example, but it is in the same domain and is focussed on end user programming.
