Steele and Sussman argue the exact opposite in The Art of the Interpreter or the Modularity Complex (Parts Zero, One, and Two).

They say that a good abstraction has a meaning independent of its implementation and is not just an abbreviation.

"I can't figure out what this is all about until I read an impl" – is just an example of poor tool choice/application (or maybe just poor perception/pov).
