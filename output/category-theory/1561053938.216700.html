# Bartosz Milewski 2019-06-22 21:45
The identity morphism is not just nominated as such. It is identified by the property that, when composed with any other morphism it gives you back that morphism. So (+1) is not identity because, if you follow it with isEven (from Int to Bool) it’s not the same as isEven:

isEven . (+1) \= isEven

Names matter in as much as they distinguish between morphisms, but you are free to rename morphisms, as long as you don’t change the composition rules. Composition is everything!
