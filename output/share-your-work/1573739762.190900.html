# Joe Lesko 2019-11-14 16:34
It's hard to articulate, but I'll try. :slightly_smiling_face: For me, Glitch's brand is friendly but the visuals are still just graphical and decorative. Storyscript feels more illustrative, and the Inkie character is very prominent -- there is a bigger gulf between storybook illustrations and the tech language being used. For instance, in the book, you jump from a story for 5-year-olds ,to phrases like "Automated infrastructure configuration, scheduling and management." It's like creating a power tool with Fisher Price aesthetics -- the tool might be amazing, but there is a dissonance there. Which is different from making a power tool with colorful ergonomic rubber handles and a plain English user manual. Still friendly but more in line with the target audience. Just my opinion though.
