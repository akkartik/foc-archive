# Stefan Lesser 2019-09-05 02:12
For the US this is even more important, as they have bred polarization with their political system for centuries. With a two-party system and every election structured like a sports event, you easily push everyone into a polarized Us-vs.-Them mentality. Those who can see beyond the simple good-vs.-evil storylines are a minority and they mistakenly hope that everyone is capable and interested in seeing the bigger picture, but unfortunately that’s not how the world works. Simple-and-wrong trumps complex-and-true all the time.

The media (and not just the “social” kind) have been riding on that wave for ages — what polarizes causes clicks and page views and engagement or whatever today’s metric to optimize for is.

And that’s where we technologists share at least partial responsibility. Creating value with effort and giving it away for free to the masses but only through recapturing that value manyfold with a business model that separates users and customers and lets one exploit the other is genius, but unfortunately also undermines our democracy.
# Stefan Lesser 2019-09-05 02:39
What we need to do — as you ask for our role in this — is to first accept that there will always be unintended consequences. We need to take responsibility for these (no, it’s not just a tool anymore and not only the user decides if it’s used for good or for evil) and design our systems with this responsibility in mind.

We need to stop believing in technological solutions for what are essentially people problems. No, you can’t fix that with a blockchain!

And we need programmers that are brave enough to show integrity in difficult situations where they are not afraid to risk their status and their jobs when they deny participation in the design and implementation of technologies that create more problems than they solve. A little bit like these 21 UK conservatives: https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2019/09/04/british-c…

As our skill set is still extremely valuable and we don’t have to worry about automation as much as those who we automate away we have quite some power in this game.
What you work on and who you work for is at this point probably more important than who you vote for. Choose wisely. Good luck!
