A new to me paper (book chapter), "Toward a Critical Technical Practice" from Philip A. Agre.

And then also, another paper that works to digest and clarify by exploring its use, "Critical Technical Practices as a Methodology for Values in Design" from Kirsten Boehner, Shay David, Joseph 'Jofish' Kaye and Phoebe Sengers

I was struck by these pieces because, if I had to say I'm anything (I don't) I'd probably claim to be a digital humanities person (I'm not); but I don't like digital humanities writ-large because as a practice it seems pretty clearly divided into "I wrote this code" or "I thought really hard about this code" where those 2 things are mutually exclusive acts. Critical Technical Practice suggests a way to do both and that both are actually both. Both. Both at the same time...because they're kinda the same thing.
# Eli Mellen 2026-03-09 07:49
A more cogent description of Critical Technical Practice, from "Critical Technical Practices as a Methodology for Values in Design"

> CTP is outlined by Phil Agre in his 1997 book Computation and Human Experience. He proposes using insights from a field that provides critical reflection to question the core metaphors of a technical field, and thereby overcome recurrent technical impasses in that field. Briefly, CTP consists of the following moves: identifying the core metaphors of the field, noticing what, when working within those metaphors, remains marginalized, inverting the dominant metaphors to bring that margin to the center, and embodying the alternative as a new technology. It is important to note that during this process, the values embodied by the field can be questioned and shifted.
