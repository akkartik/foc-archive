# Ivan Reese 2022-12-16 15:28
> what is in the physical doodads that couldn't be implemented in VR or AR
Tactile feedback? The ability to specify constraints like "these blocks need to snap together according to a certain tiling pattern" using magnets instead of having to code up some constraints? The fact that you can make eye contact with the person across the table from you?

I mean, sure, we could eventually figure that stuff out with AR / VR. There's lots of cool potential there. But this DL-esq stuff feels (A) like a much nearer-term reality (ignoring market forces, granted) and (B) like it's pursuing a fundamentally different goal from just "fill the space around you with virtual graphics"
