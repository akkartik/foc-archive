# William Taysom 2018-10-08 09:33
Wires systems have two principle potential advantages:

(1) Annotation. You have extra space for the system to add extra information on and between nodes. There's just something intuitive about putting together a bunch of pipes and then seeing stuff flow through the pipes.

(2) Propagation in the sense that Sussman used it here https://www.infoq.com/presentations/We-Really-Dont-Know-How-To-Compute, which amounts to reusing the same pipes for different stuff, a sort of radical polymorphism akin to a monadic (or applicative) lift in the typed functional programming tradition.

These qualities don't rely on the wires really. They just often come along for the ride.
