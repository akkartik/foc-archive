# William Taysom 2026-01-27 15:01
@Federico Pereiro just making a point. It's a change in the role of, like, source code in our practice.

For example, Haskell is a programming language that was invented for use in papers, like PDFs, and only tangentially executable.

It's a matter of focus. I'll be talking to theater people about AI soon and so I'm working a metaphor for them explaining what's happening to software development now. And I think I've settled on a good one.

Software development can be like staging a play including the building of sets, the programming of lights, the buying of lights, building them from scratch, and building the stage, and the HVAC system for cooling the theater, and the whole building itself.

When it comes to the staging of a play, usually the HVAC is important but not creatively so. (You could change the temperature to freak people out, but that's usually outside the magic circle of what a play does.) So there's an excitement among some of us, different from the dread so present among other creatives, because a big chunk of what we do is dumb stuff we wish we weren't.

Starting with that there's a lot to elaborate on.

# Stefan Lesser 2026-01-28 00:45
There's a difference between translating one deterministic language into another, like going from assembly to C, and using stochastic patterns to translate ambiguous human language into a deterministic programming language. That doesn't mean either of those things is good or bad. It just means we're not just adding another layer, because that layer we're adding right now is not like the others.

> A big chunk of what we do is dumb stuff we wish we weren't.
Wacky idea: Let's redesign the dumb stuff instead of teaching a model to keep doing the dumb stuff for us.

Even wackier idea: Can AI agents help us to explore the design space?

A lot of artists are worried that now everybody can do their kind of art with AI and the world gets drowned in low-quality “art” while the good stuff still exists but becomes much harder to find.
And yet some artists will probably use AI to elevate their art, because they learn how to apply it to their process instead of just hoping to have AI do the work for them.
Let's be like those artists.
