# Kartik Agaram 2018-09-14 13:27
Hmm, I'm torn. It's possible Dave is a blub programmer [1] or a macho masochist like Robert Martin [2] (he doesn't actually say "sissy" in the post), but I find myself nodding along to this statement:

> ..what will be the limitations of your language? What can’t it do? It won’t be fast enough for certain things. You’re building an abstraction and they’re always leaky, so people will need to know what they can’t do.

Though I don't agree with this part:

> Otherwise they’ll have to learn the lower level details, which defeats the purpose of an easier language.

Knowing how an abstraction works doesn't defeat its purpose. This a classic overuse of the word "abstraction" to take on division-of-labor connotations: http://akkartik.name/post/libraries

But it's super important to know the limitations of the tools we use, and this is something most people fail at. The world doesn't really make it easy. We're all competing for attention by over-inflating claims of how easy something will be. Just start using me and all your problems will disappear. I wish we could return to a more innocent world where claims were more restrained. Like the Royal Society or something..

---

Independent of this defense of Dave, I actually tend to be pretty anti-abstraction myself, so this debate is welcome. One possible entry point into my belief system:

https://lobste.rs/s/zyomiu/what_does_it_mean_design_software_well#c_yvvkwm
