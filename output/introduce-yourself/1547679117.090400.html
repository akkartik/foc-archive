# Gregor 2019-01-16 14:51
Hey'all, I've been hovering this Slack for a few months and wanted to wait with introducing myself until I have something to show. But since I just keep pushing the date, I'll cut the crap and say hi: Hi :wave: Also I just started listening to @Steve Krouse's podcast and it's been eerie (and fulfilling) to hear so similar thoughts.
My thinking for the last few years has been around more incremental improvements to the programming status quo. ~2 years ago I built a JSON editor that doesn't let you syntax error (https://gregoor.github.io/syntactor). It was supposed to be a full JS editor, but I drowned in TODOS. I talked about the project at the Salon des Refuses of that year (and met @Philip Tchernavskij, nice to see you here :slightly_smiling_face: ).
I've restarted the project a couple of months ago and I'm close to having it in a state worth showing. It's not super out there (you're still "just writing" JavaScript) but I think it's a step out of the text-manipulation stone age we currently live in.
In my day job I work as the lead developer on Mozilla's Common Voice (https://voice.mozilla.org/en). Leave your voice if you want to contribute to an open data set :upside_down_face: I live in Berlin, so if you're around ping me, and I'll show you some great vietnamese restaurant. :ramen:
