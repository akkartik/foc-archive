# Kartik Agaram 2020-02-18 12:59
Having done grad school myself, I think it's overrated how realistic it is for research. I certainly benefited from it, don't get me wrong. But from a purely research perspective it's not clear to me that staying in academia is the better option for everyone who wants to do research. Particularly if the things you want to study don't have established 'grooves' already carved out. All the most interesting subjects of study, in other words :smile:

Ignoring selfish considerations, the ideal route may be 4 years in grad school, then dropping out. That should give you all the benefits (free time, access to intellectual breadth, afternoon seminars, library) and minimize the drawbacks (pressure to publish, pressure to fundraise, pressure to conform to established research paths, darwinian selection for tenure-track positions).
