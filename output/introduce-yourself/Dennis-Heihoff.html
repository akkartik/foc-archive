> tell us about your background
Originally I got into coding through film. I was preparing for auditions for my first feature. Upon posting a casting notice online I got over 4000 submissions. I had hardly any $$ so I couldn’t hire casting agents or assistants. At that time I started to dabble in coding, mostly making small games like battleship. I realized I could use google sheets + gscript (JS-like scripting around gsuite products) to send emails. I exported all actors data into a CSV, imported it into sheets, rated actors based on their video reels and fit for the role.
Then wrote a 80 line script that sent them personalized emails in which, if their rating was high, they’d be invited to a scheduled audition and when low to an open call. The emails were signed by a made up person “Janina Kebera - Production Office Assistant.” I hacked on the script for 3 days, then pressed a button and it sent 4000+ emails in less than a second.
I got 20 responses in 2 minutes, 40 in 5. I ended up doing the auditions but never the movie. My experience with it was so impactful, I decided to pursue coding instead.
Since then I made Vimsical, root, genetic algorithms for CSS, trading algorithms, GRID and a few other projects.

> what your interests are
While learning about genetic algorithms I stumbled upon the concept of fitness functions and made my own. It’s “have fun and be curious.” This gets me around.

> how you got into computer futurism
