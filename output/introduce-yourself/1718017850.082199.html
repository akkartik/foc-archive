# agnes cameron 2024-06-10 04:10
hello, i’m agnes — I work as a technician and a lecturer at university of the arts london’s creative computing institute, and also work on games/writing/art-related things.

I spend a lot of time thinking about how people develop a sense of agency in relation to technology. I recently came across the tadi web ‘slippy mindset’ page + thought it was a great articulation of something I often try to get across in my teaching and technical work — that there’s always more than one way to do something.

I’m also really interested in collective knowledge structures and tools for describing/realising them — I’ve run a reading group for a few years now called the Knowledge Arrangement Reading Group (it’s an open group if anyone here wants to join) + am also an enthusiastic (if intermittent) wikipedia editor.

right now, I’m doing a technical writing fellowship with Bellingcat around diverse satellite imaging indices, thinking a lot about ‘meta-methods’ for thinking about imaging as a physical process. I’m also just starting a research project around developing knitting software, and also learning to make DIY synths (in the autumn I’m going to run a ‘circuit diagram reading group’ at the CCI where we make synthesisers together from scratch).

I think it’s really cool that this space exists! If anyone is based in london and wants to come visit the CCI / hang out and talk about coding things lmk :slightly_smiling_face:
