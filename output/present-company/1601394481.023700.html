# Drewverlee 2020-10-02 05:51
To give something a new name with no origin: synthetic. Example:

I called my new math concept a bimlond.


Now, I'm against doing this unless your at the category theory level. where that's kind of the point. It's better, for learning, to have conflicts and resolve them through context.

For instance, calling it "hand"to convey it moves things but doesn't make choices is better because we learn through association. It's trivial to remember that in context this doesn't refer to a human hand.
