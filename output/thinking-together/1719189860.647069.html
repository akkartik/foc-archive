# Beni Cherniavsky-Paskin 2024-07-01 11:41
The Art of Unix Programming has "art" in the name but more as "art not science", not literally as "art criticism". Nevertheless, it has quite a lot of opinionated deconstruction of what makes various things work well (or not), and more importantly work well together. "things" here being broader than strictly "tools", also of conventions/formats/protocols and such...
So no not quite what you asked, but a step in roughly that direction(?)

(I had related memories of The Unix Programming Environment but leafing through it again, it has much less subjective/philosophical content than I thought. Some short reflections at end of each chapter. It did somehow convey the "Unix way" but more by demonstration & osmosis than by explicitly talking of it?)
