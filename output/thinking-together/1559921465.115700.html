I am struggling to find a clip from a talk that I think is relevant to support what I am saying. I think it is possibly in an Eve retrospective by Chris Granger. Basically the speaker talks about demoing a "new programming" type product to highly intelligent non-programmers. The user was easily able to whip up something fairly complex (so far so good!) but then they ran into a problem where they said something like (pointing) "I want this value... here".

The value they wanted "there" was calculated deep inside nested functions and there was no way to "just move that value there" without a massive refactor.

The speaker talks about how deflating this experience was because they realised it wasn't even easy to explain why they couldn't do that without introducing a ton of concepts like scope and stacks and whatever i.e. exactly what they wanted to avoid!

I have experienced similar things with my wife who is clever and accomplished in a non-programming field. I can't even explain to her why I'm finding something difficult. I'll just be like (I need these numbers to in this window). This is not the same as algorithm challenges like Google Code Jam, where she can immediately understand why it is fundamentally hard to describe the solution, and might even be able to describe a solution with words and pictures herself.
