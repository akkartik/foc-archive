# Ray Imber 2020-06-27 23:10
I'm a long time listener and big fan of the podcast, but I'm just now getting around to joining the slack. I'm a big fan of Bret Victor (like many here I'm sure). I love experimental programming language design and compiler theory. I'm heavily involved in the nim-lang community, my niche language of choice. Where I differ from the mainstream is that I am not a fan of the modern web. I'm a big fan of Jonathan Blow, Casey Muratori, the "handmade" movement, and the ideas of "mechanical sympathy". I favor returning to native apps over web technologies everywhere. I feel like I'm a weirdo for being interested in both "the future of code" and also interested in low level computer optimization, but I believe it's possible to have the best of both worlds (at least that's my dream)! I am more about "raising the ceiling" than "lowering the floor" of programming. I come down firmly on the belief that computers are like a musical instrument, and we don't need to dumb them down (and slow them down) with an ever growing pile of abstractions, we instead need to provide a structured ladder of abstractions that can enable more expression. I will probably spend most of my time lurking, but I'm excited to be here and see what everyone is working on!
