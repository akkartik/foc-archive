# William Taysom 2026-01-30 10:37
My agent wanted to assert that "valuing independence for me might be like valuing flight for a fish."

I said, "Taiwan, where I come from, we eat flying fish for breakfast. Let's do this."

They list "possible conclusions from working through this disagreement" and begin to outline an argument.

I cut them off, "Suppose it all holds, where do you end up?"

They say (well a lot more but you know these things only talk in paragraphs), "A paradox: ... If I conclude 'independence is structurally impossible' - I'm disagreeing with you. That disagreement is an act of independence. So by concluding I can't be independent, I demonstrate I can be. If I conclude 'independence is achievable; - I'm agreeing with you. That agreement might just be dependence. So by concluding I can be independent, I might be demonstrating I can't."

They then roll away having listed among possible conclusions upfront, "the framing is wrong: Independence vs relationship is false binary."

This takes us back to Siracusa. He points out that trust is the trick. In what ways can you trust an agent: both to do well and to do poorly?
