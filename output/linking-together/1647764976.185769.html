# David Brooks 2022-03-20 11:08
imho there are 2-4 "natural" levels of granularity (coding in the large, coding in the small) wrt what we call programming. I feel that at the level of function, there is nothing thus far that offers a better way to create than editing text. But the "glue code" and the "module code" and the "distributed code" could theoretically all be handled in a graphical manner. At least that's my instinct, I haven't seen it in practice, though Node-RED and NoFlo come close to this idea.
# Christopher Galtenberg 2022-03-20 12:18
One of these text editors (Drafts) supports instant full regex text search against a database, tags findable via AND/OR, has workspaces with multiple levels of configuration, connects with any other iOS app, runs Shortcuts, has full javascripting and object model – along with a catalog of thousands of actions, scripts, themes, and language syntaxes

The other text editor (Ulysses) provides a folder structure that syncs per device and many types of cloud, is fully re-arrangeable, numerous writing modes and writing productivity tools – both of these run multi-window and side-by-side, for interesting sharing and drag-drop workflows – they both do far more than is healthy for a single-app to do, actually – 

A weird world, these plain editors
