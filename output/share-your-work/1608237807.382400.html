# Chris Knott 2020-12-17 13:16
I liked it, although it was a little hard to follow. Definitely had a little "aha" moment at 6:20 when the switch finally worked :grinning:

I think the best-in-class for this type of video is Ben Eater. Here's his latest one - https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BUTHtNrpwiI

I think he edits it so that it seems like it is live but I am sure he actually carefully scripts and narrates over it. He leaves the little mistakes in (as you did) because I think they are important for understanding.

So basically I think it should still have voiceover but you should also edit/condense it. Stuff like changing colours (which still takes quite a long time, but is conceptually boring) can be whisked past.

Other stuff, I think you need to signpost ahead of time what you are intending to do, then do, then show it working. Like: "Ok, now I'm going to try and make this platform move backwards when I flip the button. First, I'm going to need...". This will help follow the design thought process.

(Personal preference but I also found the music kind of annoying, especially when it changed)

All in all, a really great project, will follow closely!
