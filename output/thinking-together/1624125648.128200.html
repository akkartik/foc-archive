# Nick Winter 2021-06-21 09:19
Right, significant tooling would be required for any one element of making this kind of pair programming:
1. on demand, where you can have people you don’t know come and help you synchronously with no friction
2. many-to-one, where lots of people could be helping you without it being overwhelming (text-based, working with your flow instead of interrupting it)
3. skill-level-flexible, where you can’t trust that the edits your collaborators are making are good and have to have some sort of lightweight, semi-synchronous review process
If you had such tooling, though, then there seem to be many areas where this kind of assistance could be either fun (Twitch streaming, audience competing to get code snippets accepted by Jon Blow on stream) or affordable (companies/developers paying for narrow specialists to increase their velocity and code quality while they work).
