# Emil H 2021-02-23 09:56
I also like the resulting layout, let me reflect on why. I might be stating the obvious here, but anyway. Newspaper nostalgia aside, I like how you found a meaningful use for the advancement in screen technology. This layout would never have worked back on a 640*480 CRT, but today I'm on a 27 inch high-res screen (almost as large as a newspaper?), and yet many modern apps & websites just waste that space, they use it for whitespace/margins and slightly smoother icons. Instead, you are fitting more content on screen! Browsing different articles by moving my gaze is inherently faster and easier than browsing by clicking a link and waiting for a new page to load.

And I like that it works on phone too. The headlines are large enough to read, and then I can zoom in to read the rest. Double-tapping in Chrome on Android also does... some kind of zoom, maybe not exactly a "column zoom" like iOS, but still useful in this layout. Regarding ease-of-use, zooming/panning is in the same ballpark as tapping a link to a new article.

As a side effect, I got to read some interesting articles on your site, thanks!
