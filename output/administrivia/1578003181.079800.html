# Ivan Reese 2020-01-02 14:13
Another point in favor of chat with threads (Slack), instead of flat chat with named topics (Zulip) or a forum (Discourse) — When you're scrolling through the list of top-level messages in slack, with the various threads spun off of each, those top-level messages are quite rich, with images and video widgets and URLs and the full content of the message. In a forum, your index isn't nearly as rich — it's typically just the short titles of threads, and maybe a few words of preview text from the message body. Same with Zulip, your index (list of topics in the sidebar) is just a very short summary. This means any enticement you feel by the thread/topic needs to come from the wording of the title, which is often a terrible indicator.

Reddit seems to have struck a good balance here. Too bad their web UI is so frustrating to use.
