# Stefan Lesser 2018-11-30 13:20
I’ve just been reading Lakoff’s Metaphors We Live By and was shocked to realize how much math and science in general and programming specifically are designed around that particular world view of objectivism. The way we built them, computers today are stuck to be extreme rational objectivists — in a way it even explains why we still mostly have a hard time ascribing any creative properties to a machine (although that’s just slowly changing with some art emerging from the latest breakthroughs in machine learning).

I always thought we are just searching for the right abstraction (aka truth) when we model systems, and if it turns out bad we just haven’t found the right one. But the experientialist model described in Lakoff’s book opens up a whole new world of possibilities. Now I’m wondering how we can apply the idea of metaphorical structuring to systems design. We seem to be pretty good at it with language, which I consider proof that it’s possible.
