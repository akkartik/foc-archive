# Ivan Reese 2022-03-26 10:13
We're opening a new channel: #reading-together

This channel will be host to in-depth discussions of FoC-relevant academic papers and other long, substantial, or deep materials that deserve thorough consideration and reflection. It'll also be a place to request these sorts of materials — if you're looking for papers on a specific topic, post a request in this channel and the audience there might be able to help you find some.

Here's how it fits in with our existing channels:

#thinking-together — Deep, substantial discussions emerging from your own thoughts on the future of programming.
#reading-together — Deep, substantial discussions emerging from FoC-related books or academic papers, and requests for such.
#share-your-work — Links to FoC-related materials that you created, with the discussion focused on positive critique and encouraging feedback.
#linking-together — Links to FoC-related materials that you didn't create, shared for general/casual interest.
#present-company — All other discussions/links that might be of interest to the community, even if not directly pertinent to the future of programming.

Why are the channels divided in this way? To help the audience find something interesting to read or discuss. Each channel has a different tenor—a different kind of signal/noise—and when they're mixed together they tend to drown each other out. This plurality of channels places a bit of additional burden on those who post — you need to make sure you're posting to a channel that is compatible with what you're posting. When in doubt, just give it your best guess — though you can always DM me to ask, too.
