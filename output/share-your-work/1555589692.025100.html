# Kartik Agaram 2019-04-18 10:26
The quality of the write-up is excellent.

I'm skeptical of "Reading a couple hundred books a year is the bare minimum. It’s just the baseline." It's the baseline to get to where Alan Kay is now, for better and for worse. But I suspect you need more than Alan Kay to get where you're going. Too much book learning could be where he went astray. At a couple hundred books per year you'd finish his recommendations in a year and still have time to do things in later years. Or you could read 20 books per year for ten years and actually play with some of the ideas you're reading about.

I don't mean to say that Alan Kay is just reading books without doing anything, year in and year out. Just using contrast for rhetorical purpose. I don't actually know what missteps projects like Dynabook or STEPS may have made. But it's worth keeping those possibilities on your radar to avoid survivor bias in your thinking.

# Gregg Tavares 2019-05-01 03:06
Sorry I think I ranted on this before but I vehemently disagree with making the web kernel smaller. I used to have that opinion and I changed my mind.

I'm assuming by that people mean they just want some low-level VM like thing with pixels and input. So let's consider some of the issues

• Everyone renders their own text using their own font data and their own font renderers.

Result: Internationalization is worse than ever. Trying to type Chinese, Japanese, Korean, fails in 99% of apps.

• Everyone renders text to pixels

Result: No way to write ad blockers since there is no structure to the data. It's just a block of pixels. No way to write translation extensions like Rikaikun. No way for Google Translate to translate a page because it can't scan the document. At best it could only OCR what's on the screen at the moment assuming the page doesn't have some fancy background making OCR too hard. No way to select, copy and paste text. Again there's no standard structure so unless the app itself decides to implement highlighting text with the cursor and letting you copy that text to a clipboard you're out of luck.

• No blocking videos

Videos are currently blockable because they are generally implemented using a <video> tag and because the protocols are standardized. In a 'we draw video using our own codec to pixel world' no more blocking video since there is no way for the system to know that you're drawing pixels for text or video, it's all just pixels.

• No support for Accessibility tech

Again, because the common format is HTML it's easy to write something that translates to braille or reads the words aloud. In a "it's a bucket of pixels and all data is opaque" that disappears

• Hyperlinks disappear

If everyone is making up their own display to pixels tech then there are no links

There are many many similar issues. I know browsers have their issues but I disagree the world would be better with a low-level "cross platform assembly + drawing surface"

I also know some of the issues above could be kind of solved by standards and praying that every developer in the world follows those standards and that they all update to the new standards as the standards change.

My impression is that by having the standard be the default method of creation it leads to far more people following the standards since it's the path of least resistance.
