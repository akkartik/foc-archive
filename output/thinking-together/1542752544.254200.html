# William Taysom 2018-11-20 17:11
Where to begin... I don't have a concise answer having come at things the long way around. I suppose the Curry-Howard correspondence is one place to start https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Curry%E2%80%93Howard_correspondence, just learning that to some degree programming language constructs correspond to logical ones, that there's something fundamental at work rather than just a bunch of arbitrary choices. Take that thought train to the end of a line and you may end up at Algebraic Databases https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.03501. Spivak has a fun survey text too https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.05316. Can give you a feel for the approach without assuming so much.

# Kartik Agaram 2018-11-20 17:52
Eric Hehner's "A Practical Theory of Programming" hits my radar every once in a while. I've read the first third or so and it's pretty good. Has a fairly extensive theory of data structures (unlike my bible, Dijkstra's "A Discipline of Programming"). There's a free pdf at http://www.cs.toronto.edu/~hehner/aPToP. One warning, however: he's not concerned with issues of efficiency.
