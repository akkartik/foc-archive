# Parris 2019-09-12 17:03
@Ivan Reese ooo that is def surprising and interesting! No keyframe animations or ability to draw artwork unfortunately (yet). We do have some pre-canned animations, and we wouldn't be too far off from letting you build your own animations up from scratch.

As of right now our interactivity is mostly focused on showing/hiding things, and screen navigation. We allow for branching or linear (scrolling up/down or swiping left/right) screen navigation.

We may not be well suited if you often create characters and do a lot of animation in Flash. Buttttt you have inspired me. Would love to hear more about why your team still uses Flash. # Edward de Jong 2019-09-15 00:36
A lot of the Flash bad reputation was nasty propaganda from Adobe hating companies, which included almost everyone. Adobe makes great stuff, but so many other companies feared it. Microsoft and Apple both did battle with Adobe's creativity tools, and often came up on the losing end. MS spent untold hundreds of millions building clones of Flash/Shockwave (their Silverlight), and tried to build publishing tools, etc., and Apple also had a fantasy that their Pages would somehow hold up against InDesign. MS and Apple teamed up to make TrueType to counter Adobe's Type 1 font format which they didn't want to pay for. Lots of rivalries between these companies, and lots of FUD (fear uncertainty and doubt) press releases sponsored by the negative-evangelical arms of those firms. Not many people acknowledge the existence of those kinds of PR staffers, but they do exist. I am personally sick of people pretending that everyone in the valley is playing sports like a proper english gentleman. There are tons of dirty tricks, and the bigger the company the dirtier they get IMHO. Who can forget Intel paying companies huge bonuses if they ship only 100% intel parts. that is so purely anticompetitive, they should have put intel CEO in jail.
