yeah, actually interest in webcode comes from dissatisfaction with the expressivity of node-red. node-red is primarily low-code node and wires interface, but webcode is definitely a code based approach, a.k.a. aa yes-code platform (but without the BS of setting up local tools and an out-of-the-box SOTA development workflow).

I think this will mean webcode/observable will be able to produce high quality user facing dashboards that would be quite tedious and difficult to produce with node-red.
