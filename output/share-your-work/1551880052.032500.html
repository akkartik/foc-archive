# Edward de Jong 2019-03-06 19:41
I thought you did a very good job at creating a rudimentary 5 pane tiled interface. If you wish i can give you some feedback on the interface, where numerous sub-optimal decisions were made. One of the biggest problems with the project is that in the case of the square being redrawn you picked an application which is too simple to be a good test. I suggest you pick a series of apps, in a progression like clock, wristwatch, snake, tictactoe, minesweeper, chess. You can't tell how good an idea is at the "hello world" level, you have to take on a tougher problem to see how it pans out.
# Edward de Jong 2019-03-06 19:45
Direct manipulation of moving something on the screen has almost zero value in programming. That is not where bugs come from; that is mostly aesthetic iteration, which is one of the easiest parts of programming. Sure it saves a little bit of time, but since debugging is 80%+ of programming, how much has been saved by direct manipulation of an existing graphic?. Trying to correct an order-of-initialization issue can take huge amounts of time, and that is something that apparently graphical manipulation is very weak at fixing.
