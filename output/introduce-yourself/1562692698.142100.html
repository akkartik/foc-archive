# Alan Zaffetti 2019-07-09 10:18
Hi all! Longtime follower and recent contributor on the “robust first” computing effort with @Dave Ackley. My background is working in the industry on large software systems. I’m determined to build life-like robustness into a new computing stack. As a concrete analogy: I like to think of how primitive cellular functions build upon each other to achieve higher level goals. This could be similar to how traditional software functions are composed today. The best part is we get to start from scratch. Removing billions of years of cruft :-) we can build crisp and predictable functions with good reuse. That’s part of the fun and challenge of this work, and we’re definitely still at the beginning. Would love to get to know you all and answer questions!

Edit: I hang out in: #robust-computation and http://gitter.im/t2tile/ is where most of the real work happens.

# Alan Zaffetti 2019-07-09 11:47
@Steve Krouse I don’t have a quick answer for you unfortunately. Our home on the web is still Gitter which is more of an long form chat. There’s the [YouTube video series](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC1M91QuLZfCzHjBMEKvIc-A) perhaps one of the early episodes can answer some things. There’s always @Dave Ackley too.

T2 tackles building real hardware (the tile) that can implement some of the ideas and previous theory. (In no particular order):

  • resistance to hardware nondetermism and faults, under arbitrary failure modes
  • indefinite scalability
  • robustness via artificial life principles.

    Admittedly I’m not active with the hardware side.
    • # Dave Ackley 2019-07-09 12:05
    Hi @Steve Krouse. The goal of the T2 tile project is to demonstrate a strictly indefinitely scalable computer architecture, and use the resulting system to set an initial benchmark record for the 'Average Event Rate' (AER) that such an architecture can achieve. Perhaps the fastest way into the goals and concepts is the 2016 AAAI paper (5pg Q&A) at https://www.aaai.org/ocs/index.php/AAAI/AAAI16/paper/download/11987/12203
