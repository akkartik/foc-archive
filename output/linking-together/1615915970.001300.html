# Christopher Galtenberg 2021-03-16 12:29
In practice people learn languages symbolically regardless, whether the syntax is "field of button" or "button.field" – 

When I was 12, knowing hypertalk's "send message 'abc' to card 'foo'" and then encountering C's foo->abc() , it clicked immediately and my reaction was "cool!" – so for me at least it worked

Parsing is the hyperscript programmer's problem, keeping a sane grammar – and others can probably hack around that grammar for a less talky syntax in the future
# Andrew F 2021-03-16 15:34
Parsing is everyone's problem. The user needs a fairly accurate mental model of how the parser works to be able to read or write their code. They have to parse it mentally to read it, and code is read more often than it's written.
