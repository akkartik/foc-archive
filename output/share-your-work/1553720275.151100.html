# Edward de Jong 2019-03-27 16:06
i see your clock program. Not too far from Beads so can't be too critical hah. I do however think that the usage of [# space] to be a comment and [## space] to be a higher level comment is understandable, but having # prefix for an identifier to mean a tracked variable seems ill-advised. Why not use one of the other dozen punctuation characters like @ or % as a significant prefix. Seems way too dangerous to overload the comment delimiter with a functional prefix, when the only difference is a small space character. Makes it much harder to read; you are trying to train the eye to skip over the comments quickly and this will impede that training. So my criticism is about ergonomics. This is one of the things i hate about languages which overload the + operator. So plus can mean in some languages vector addition, string concatenation, all sorts of stuff. In FORTH you could redefine the + operator which was insane.
