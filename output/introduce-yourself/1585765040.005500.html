# Dan Cook 2020-04-02 16:54
I say software is ultimately never been about the code. Knowing what makes a "good home" has nothing to do with understanding the properties of wood under different conditions. I mean, yes that's vital information for process of construction, but not of the actual design and purpose. The proper process is to make the design easy to do, and then let someone or something else (whose job it is to deal with that) deal with that. I don't think that's any less valid in software.
# Dan Cook 2020-04-02 17:02
... although it gets muddy where you draw the line between "code" and "logic", which is part of the design of software. You have to be able to specify what the software is and what it does. If "code" is that stuff you feed to a compiler, then there's no fundamental reason why the specification has to be in the form of "code" in that sense. But if "code" means a specification of data and behaviors, well then it's all the same thing. But at that rate, any tool or user interface constitutes a "language", and whatever model or info you put into it constitutes "code".

Personally, I agree that it's all code, and that "no code" just means, not what people traditionally associate with as being "code" ("none of that stuff")
