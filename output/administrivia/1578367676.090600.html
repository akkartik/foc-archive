# Jonathan Edwards 2020-01-07 07:47
@Ivan Reese LtU is a PL theorist subculture. I think what happened is it went mainstream with the success of FP. Nowadays you can give talks on dependent types and proof assistants at StrangeLoop or CurryOn or a whole host of FP-related events, many with “lambda” in their name as the dog whistle. The decline of LtU is really a sign of success, so I wouldn’t be that worried about the same fate befalling FoC.
# Kartik Agaram 2020-01-07 08:37
They always had a light cordon between the front page and the group forums. Someone would have to explicitly suggest a post be 'promoted'. Which meant that even at its height all the action was in the forum, which newcomers could miss. For as long as I can remember Ehud Lamm would periodically call for people to promote stories to the front page. But nobody seemed to notice that it was vestigial.
