# Pine Wu 2019-09-27 08:58
I'd say conference lacks communication between speakers / attendees whereas symposium focuses more on exchange of ideas? I have spoken at conferences and I feel 1) you need to cut more niche / deep contents to accommodate all attendees 2) conversations can only happen in the 10 min breaks or after party and many attendees don't even get the chance to chat with speakers in more depth.

If I'm presenting I'd be very happy to assume attendees are excited about the same kind of ideas I'm interested in, and to know I can have a deeper than over-the-beer conversations afterwards with them.
# Breck Yunits 2019-09-27 09:32
I was at the Programming Synthesis conference last week, which maybe was more like a symposium. I would say there were ~50 folks there and ~20 spoke either for a few minutes or longer sessions. I think it would be fun to design a smallish conference (fewer than 100, perhaps as few as 50), and have each person speak for perhaps 2 minutes each, about what they are working on/most passionate about. That way you maximize the odds of everyone connecting with the people most relevant to their work, and you can spend most time in conversation and less time sitting in seats listening
