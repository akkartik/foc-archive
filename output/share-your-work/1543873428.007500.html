# Joshua Horowitz 2018-12-03 21:39
Kinda tangental, but: you can take the existence of "reduce" as the definition of a list.

Usually you define a list with something like:

List a = Nil | Cons a (List a)

This is the "positive" definition of a list, in terms of constructors. But there's a dual, "negative" definition in terms of eliminators:

List a = ∀b. (b, (a, b) → b) → b

What's that weird term on the RHS? It's the signature of a generic reduce function!

Turning the positive definition into the negative definition is a totally mechanical process, which I'm pretty sure is called Church encoding. This connection might look slightly more clear if I write:

List a = ∀b. (() → b, (a, b) → b) → b

"() → b" says what the reduce does to Nil, and "(a, b) → b" says what the reduce does to Cons. Every constructor turns into a parameter to the reduce.
