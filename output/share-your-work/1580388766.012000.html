# Mariano Guerra 2020-02-01 10:29
Me and a friend of mine gave twice a course in our city in argentina to woman without prior programming experience. It's a side thing, not related with instadeq at all. The first two courses where done with a written curriculum (here you can check it, it's in spanish but you can see the screenshots, code and demos https://creemosenlaweb.github.io/) we started with mozilla thimble since it was online and interactive, but they closed it down so we migrated to glitch which is ok but a little bit too heavy.
# Mariano Guerra 2020-02-01 10:32
one of the objectives was no setup at all, just a modern browser and instant feedback from the changes, we tried to go small steps and always showing the results. I think the syntax made more than one "not see the forest" because they were learning about how to structure a document at the same time they were learning the strange ways our tools expect us to tell them about the document we want
# Mariano Guerra 2020-02-01 10:33
but we also didn't wanted to use tools that would ease the development of websites but that were unrelated with what's used in reality, so no wird dsls or tools that don't map to html/css/js
