# Arsalan Bashir 2018-12-20 07:06
Hi all, I’m building a non-linear reactive document for the masses! And it’s called “AfterClass”. AfterClass is right now is specifically for founders of early stage startups who want to save time and money by automating every day repetitive tasks connected with their documents, like sending and keeping track of invoices, or asking the team for updates and logging those in a document, or even automatically pulling data from your other apps (Google Analytics, Quickbooks, etc) and compiling a weekly report and sending it to your email.

I like to think of it as “Zapier meets Google Docs”

My end goal is to create a dynamic digital medium that is more tied in with the real world by means of being non-linear and reactive, as opposed to static and linear documents.

I am aware that this is a massive problem, but here’s step one — https://afterclass.co

I’d be happy to hear your feedback and comments

# Arsalan Bashir 2018-12-20 08:19
I love Notion, and use it daily, but it seems more suited for storing notes (they have a fresh take on non-linear notebooks) and creating documents around their block structure. With AfterClass, I intend each document to be accessed from a single pane. The idea is to eliminate the need for recursive google searches and “document overload” that happens when you have a quickly growing “tree” of documents.

Coda is doing amazing things with automations, but it’s still built around the table structure. I’m trying to combine a block-based structure with custom automations that you can create yourself. With Coda, you can have, say a button that integrates with a specific API, but I want people to mix and match different actions and triggers to create their own automations.
